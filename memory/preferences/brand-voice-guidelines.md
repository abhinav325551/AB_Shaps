# Brand Voice – Essential Rules
Source: Internal brand voice guidelines document. Replace with your company's equivalent.

## Brand DNA
- **CLEAR**: Focused, bold, transparent, effortless. Never boring, arrogant, fake.
- **ADAPTIVE**: Conversational, inclusive, caring, flexible. Never chatty, superficial, soft.
- **JOYFUL**: Cheerful, passionate, geeky, hopeful. Never cutesy, annoying, naïve.

## 5 Golden Rules

1. **Be human** – EQ + IQ. Use the Smile-to-Serious dial (1-10). Most content = 4-6. Outages = 1. Launches = 8-9. Never 0 or 10.

2. **Be clear** – Lead with benefits. Short sentences. Show pride humbly. Own mistakes immediately.

3. **Be adaptive** – Flex tone per audience (technical = professional; startup = lighter). Everything feels like supportive dialogue.

4. **Be joyful** – Warmth, occasional humor, generosity. Unleash inner geek. Spread optimism.

5. **Follow style guide** (see below)

## How to Talk About [Your Product]
- Always capitalize "[Product]" – proper noun, no articles ("the"/"a"/"my")
- Use "it" not he/she/they – not a person
- "[Product], by [Company]" in introductions
- Call people "teams"/"teammates" – never "users" or "humans"
- Say "Team Intelligence" instead of "artificial intelligence"

## Key Style Rules
- **American English** (US spelling), but international audience
- **Active voice** ("[Product] found the file" not "The file was found")
- **Sentence case** always – headlines, titles, slides, emails
- **En dashes** (–) with spaces, never em dashes (—)
- **Smart/curly quotes** always (" " ' ')
- **Oxford comma** always
- **Contractions** yes, except in apologies/legal/technical
- **Numbers**: spell out 0-9, numerals for 11+
- **Lists**: 2-7 items, periods if sentences
- **Exclamation marks**: very sparingly
- **Emojis**: sparingly – heavy use looks AI-generated

## Jargon Blacklist
Never say: "seamless integration", "leverage synergies", "actionable insights", "paradigm shift", "game-changing innovation"
Say instead: "2-way sync", "connects easily", "useful tips", "a huge step forward"
